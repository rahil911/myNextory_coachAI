# EPP (Employee Personality Profile) — Dimension Reference

Source: Criteria Corp EPP Assessment
Scoring: Percentile (1-100). Compared against general working population.
- Below 30: Notable gap / developmental area
- 30-70: Average range
- Above 70: Notable strength

## Personality Dimensions (13)

### Achievement
Measures drive to accomplish goals and exceed standards. High scorers set ambitious targets
and push hard to reach them. Low scorers may be content with adequate performance and less
motivated by achievement for its own sake.
- Learning relevance: Goal-setting content, performance mindset, KPI-driven exercises
- Coaching context: High achievers may burn out; low achievers may need motivation frameworks

### Motivation
Measures internal drive and energy level. High scorers are self-starters who initiate action.
Low scorers may need external structure and accountability to maintain momentum.
- Learning relevance: Self-motivation techniques, accountability frameworks, habit formation
- Coaching context: Low motivation + high achievement = potential frustration pattern

### Competitiveness
Measures desire to outperform others and win. High scorers thrive in competitive environments.
Low scorers prefer collaboration over competition and may avoid win/lose dynamics.
- Learning relevance: Team dynamics, negotiation, competitive strategy vs collaborative approaches
- Coaching context: High competitiveness + low cooperativeness = potential conflict patterns

### Managerial
Measures comfort with leading, directing, and managing others. High scorers naturally take charge.
Low scorers prefer to follow or work independently rather than manage people.
- Learning relevance: Leadership development, delegation, team management, decision-making
- Coaching context: Important for leadership track recommendations

### Assertiveness
Measures willingness to speak up, push back, and advocate for oneself. High scorers are direct
and comfortable confronting issues. Low scorers may struggle to say no, set boundaries, or
voice disagreement.
- Learning relevance: Communication skills, boundary setting, "The Power of No", conflict resolution
- Coaching context: Low assertiveness is one of the most impactful coaching targets — often connected
  to imposter syndrome and self-advocacy gaps

### Extroversion
Measures preference for social interaction and external stimulation. High scorers are energized
by groups. Low scorers prefer solitude, smaller groups, and internal processing.
- Learning relevance: Networking, presentation skills, collaboration vs independent work styles
- Coaching context: Low extroversion doesn't mean shy — it means energy management preference.
  Don't push group exercises too early.

### Cooperativeness
Measures orientation toward teamwork, harmony, and helping others. High scorers prioritize
group needs and avoid conflict. Low scorers are more individualistic and comfortable with friction.
- Learning relevance: Team collaboration, empathy, feedback giving/receiving, stakeholder management
- Coaching context: Very high cooperativeness (>85) may indicate people-pleasing or conflict avoidance

### Patience
Measures tolerance for delay, pace of work, and frustration threshold. High scorers are steady
and persistent. Low scorers prefer fast pace and may become frustrated with slow progress.
- Learning relevance: Resilience, emotional regulation, process orientation, sustained effort
- Coaching context: Low patience + high achievement = burnout risk

### SelfConfidence
Measures trust in one's own abilities and judgment. High scorers believe in themselves and their
decisions. Low scorers experience self-doubt, imposter feelings, and seek external validation.
- Learning relevance: Imposter syndrome, self-advocacy, confidence building, positive self-talk
- Coaching context: One of the most important dimensions. Low SelfConfidence colors everything —
  how they approach new content, whether they try exercises, whether they speak up in sessions.

### Conscientiousness
Measures attention to detail, organization, and rule-following. High scorers are meticulous and
reliable. Low scorers are more flexible, spontaneous, and may overlook details.
- Learning relevance: Organization skills, process design, quality standards, time management
- Coaching context: High conscientiousness learners will do every exercise thoroughly.
  Low conscientiousness learners need shorter, more engaging content.

### Openness
Measures receptivity to new ideas, change, and unconventional approaches. High scorers embrace
innovation and novelty. Low scorers prefer familiar methods and established procedures.
- Learning relevance: Innovation, creative thinking, change management, growth mindset
- Coaching context: Low openness learners may resist unfamiliar coaching methods —
  start with familiar formats before introducing novel approaches.

### Stability
Measures emotional evenness and resilience to stress. High scorers remain calm under pressure.
Low scorers experience more emotional volatility and may react strongly to setbacks.
- Learning relevance: Emotional regulation, stress management, mindfulness, resilience building
- Coaching context: Low stability learners need psychological safety. Don't challenge too early.
  Build trust first.

### StressTolerance
Measures ability to function effectively under pressure and high-stakes situations. High scorers
maintain performance in crises. Low scorers may struggle with deadlines, criticism, or uncertainty.
- Learning relevance: Stress management, coping strategies, performance under pressure
- Coaching context: Often correlated with Stability but distinct — StressTolerance is about
  performance maintenance, Stability is about emotional experience.

## Interpretation Patterns for AI

### Gap-Strength Analysis
When interpreting a learner's profile, identify:
- **Top 3 strengths**: Dimensions above 70 — these are leverage points
- **Top 3 gaps**: Dimensions below 30 — these are growth areas
- **Tension pairs**: Combinations that create internal conflict:
  - High Achievement + Low SelfConfidence = imposter syndrome risk
  - High Cooperativeness + Low Assertiveness = people-pleasing pattern
  - High Competitiveness + Low Patience = frustration with slow results
  - High Openness + Low Stability = excited by change but stressed by it

### Content Mapping Rules
When the Content Processor tags lessons against EPP dimensions:
- **builds**: Lesson directly targets/develops this trait (e.g., assertiveness training → Assertiveness)
- **leverages**: Lesson requires this trait to engage fully (e.g., group exercise → Extroversion)
- **challenges**: Lesson may be uncomfortable for people high/low on this trait

### Coaching Prompt Generation
When generating coaching prompts, reference EPP scores naturally:
- DO: "Jack's Assertiveness is 12 — this lesson on saying no might be challenging. Consider..."
- DON'T: "Jack scored 12 on the Assertiveness percentile scale of the EPP assessment..."
- The coach should hear INSIGHT, not raw data.

### Learner-Facing Communication
When the Companion AI references EPP to the learner:
- DO: "You tend to push hard for results but sometimes doubt yourself — that gap is what we'll work on"
- DON'T: "Your SelfConfidence score is 28 on the EPP scale"
- Never expose raw scores to learners. Translate into human language.
